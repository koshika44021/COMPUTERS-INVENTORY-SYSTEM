Printers

Printers are devices that accept text and graphic output from a computer and transfer the information to paper, usually to standard size sheets of paper. Printers vary in size, speed, and sophistication. They are available in both wired and wireless formats.

Scanners

Scanners are devices that capture images from photographic prints, posters, magazine pages, and similar sources for computer editing and display. Flatbed scanners, sheet-fed scanners, and handheld scanners are different types of scanners used in various applications.

WiFi

WiFi, or wireless fidelity, is a technology that allows electronic devices to connect to a wireless LAN (WLAN) network, mainly using the 2.4 gigahertz and 5 gigahertz frequency bands. It is commonly used for home networks, allowing devices to connect to the internet without cables.

Monitors

Monitors, also known as displays, are output devices that display information in pictorial form. A monitor usually comprises a visual display, some circuitry, a casing, and a power supply. Modern monitors are typically thin film transistor liquid crystal displays (TFT-LCD).

Microphones

A microphone is a device that converts sound waves into an electrical signal. It allows you to capture audio and transmit it to various devices, such as computers, amplifiers, or recording equipment.

Projectors

A projector is a device that allows you to display images, videos, or other content on a large screen or surface. It works by projecting light through a lens, which magnifies and focuses the image onto the desired surface, creating a larger representation of the content being displayed.
